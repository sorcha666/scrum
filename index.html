Welcome everyone! Today, we are presenting our municipal library management system. We used the Scrum methodology to build a modern, connected network that makes library services easier for everyone.

Project Context & Objectives

Building a unified digital network

Our project brings multiple library branches into one digital system. This makes it easier to manage staff, buy new books, and move items between locations. We focused on creating a secure platform that works well for both librarians and readers.

Digitize Workflow

Move from manual tasks to a fast web platform for all branches.

Secure Access

Clear roles for Staff, Librarians, Central Service, and Readers.

Agile Success

Organized into 4 Sprints to ensure high quality and steady progress.

Currently, many libraries work separately, which makes tracking books and staff difficult. Our goal is to connect them. This project digitizes all operations, from hiring staff to managing book loans, ensuring every branch follows the same rules.

We broke the project down into five domains. This helped us create clear user stories. We cover the network setup, the digital book catalog, membership registration, the daily loaning process, and inter-branch book transfers.

Library Network Setup

Setting up branches and staff

This workflow shows how the library network starts. The Central Service creates new branches, assigns a General Secretary to manage them, and hires librarians. This step must be finished before the library can start its daily operations.

CORE FLOW

Network Setup & Governance

First, we look at the network setup. Before we can loan books, we need to build the foundation. This includes creating physical branch locations, hiring staff, and assigning a manager to oversee each site.

Book Acquisitions

Buying and distributing books

This process covers how we get new books. A professional reader reviews a book first. If approved, the Central Service buys copies and sends them to different branches. Each book gets a unique ID number automatically to avoid confusion.

ACQUISITION

Purchase & Distribution Control

Getting new books is a controlled process. A professional reader must first review and recommend a book. Once authorized, the Central office buys the copies and distributes them throughout the network. Each copy is automatically tracked with a unique ID.

Memberships

Registration and card renewals

We manage two types of readers. Local residents can sign up online for a digital card, which they must renew every year. Professional readers have a special registration process that the Central Service must check and approve.

MEMBERSHIP

Resident & Professional Portals

Membership management is simple and digital. Residents can create their cards online from home by providing their address. These cards last one year. Professional readers also have their own portal, which helps us track their expert reviews.

Loans & Returns

Managing book circulation

When a reader borrows a book, the system checks their membership and the 3-book limit. When they return it, a librarian checks the book's condition. If the book is damaged or late, the system automatically applies a fee to their account.

CIRCULATION

Loan Limits & Condition Tracking

This is the daily heart of the library. We enforce a strict limit of 3 books per reader to ensure everyone has access. On return, librarians check for damage. If a book is in poor condition, the system automatically bills a repair fee to the reader.

Inter-Branch Transfers

Moving books between libraries

Our system monitors book stocks in every library. If one branch is missing books, the Central Service orders a transfer from a branch that has extra. We use a formal transfer slip to track the books as they move through the network.

LOGISTICS

Inter-Branch Stock Balancing

The transfer process ensures that books are where they are needed most. If a branch is low on stock, we relocate copies from other branches. A formal transfer slip is created to ensure no books are lost during the move.
