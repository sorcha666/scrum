Project Context & Objectives

Why a digital library management system?

The Municipal Library Management System is designed to modernize and unify the operations of an expanding network of library branches. By transitioning to a centralized digital infrastructure, the project aims to streamline book acquisitions, enforce standardized borrowing policies, facilitate seamless inter-branch transfers, and provide an intuitive, secure portal for both library staff and readers.

Digitize Operations

Replace manual processes with a unified web platform covering all library branches.

Secure Access

Role-based authentication for Staff, Librarians, Central Service & Readers.

Agile Delivery

Structured into 4 Sprints using Scrum methodology with strict velocity control (≤32 SP).

Case Study Overview

5 functional domains extracted from the requirements document

Based on a detailed analysis of the library's operational needs, we decomposed the system into five core functional domains. These domains serve as the architectural foundation for our Use Case modeling, ensuring every aspect of the library's day-to-day management is digitally supported.

Library Network

Create branches, hire staff, assign managers, attribute unique book IDs.

Catalogue

Manage books, authors, publishers, editions, prices and genres.

Memberships

Online card creation for residents, professional reader registration.

Loans & Returns

Borrow/return tracking, 3-book limit enforcement, late fees.

Transfers

Inter-branch book transfers, deficit detection, transfer slips.

Library Network Setup

Initialization of branches and central services

This initial workflow establishes the foundational structure of the municipal library network. It covers the creation of physical branches by the Central Service, the assignment of a General Secretary (manager) to each branch, and the hiring process for librarians. This setup is a prerequisite for all subsequent library operations.

Book Acquisitions

Selection, purchasing, and distribution

The acquisition process begins with a professional reader submitting a book review. If approved by the Central Service, the book is officially authorized. The Central Service then purchases physical copies of specific editions and distributes them across the various library branches, where they are automatically assigned unique inventory numbers.

Memberships

Reader registration and card renewals

This process handles user onboarding. Local residents can register online by providing their personal information to obtain a digital library card, which must be renewed annually. Professional readers undergo a similar but distinct registration process requiring validation from the Central Service.

Loans & Returns

Managing the circulation of books

When a reader wishes to borrow a book, the system verifies their membership status and ensures they haven't exceeded the strict 3-book limit. If eligible, the loan is recorded. Upon return, the librarian inspects the book's condition. If damaged, a repair fee is applied to the reader's account.

Inter-Branch Transfers

Resolving deficits through network collaboration

To ensure equitable access, the system constantly monitors inventory across the network. If a branch is identified as having a deficit of certain books, the Central Service mandates a transfer. A transfer slip is generated, and surplus books are relocated from one branch to another to balance the catalogue.

Sprint Planning & Distribution

Strict MoSCoW ordering — Must Haves completed first

The Burndown and Velocity charts track our execution efficiency. With a total of 126 Story Points, we maintained a consistent average velocity of 31.5 SP per sprint. The steady decline in the burndown chart indicates a healthy pace, successfully absorbing the technical complexity of the centralized mainframe integration without scope creep.

The project was executed across four distinct sprints, each with a two-week duration. The velocity limit of 32 Story Points was strategically chosen based on our team of 4 developers, allocating a realistic capacity of 8 SP per person. This ensures a sustainable pace while allowing for thorough documentation and the technical complexity of the centralized mainframe integration.
